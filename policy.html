PassZap Privacy Policy

Last updated: September 1, 2025

At PassZap, we prioritize your privacy and security. Our password generator operates entirely in your browser, ensuring no data is collected, stored, or transmitted to any servers. Below, we outline how PassZap works and how we protect your information.

Data Collection and Usage

PassZap generates secure passwords with prime number digits using client-side JavaScript (window.crypto.getRandomValues). No user inputs, generated passwords, or any other data are sent to or stored on our servers. All processing happens locally in your browser, ensuring maximum privacy.

Advertisements

PassZap uses Google AdSense to display advertisements, which helps keep the service free. These ads may use cookies or similar technologies to personalize content, as managed by Google. We do not control or access these cookies. For details on how Google handles ad-related data, please review Google’s Privacy Policy.

Third-Party Links

PassZap may include links to external sites (e.g., Google’s Privacy Policy). We are not responsible for the content or privacy practices of these third-party sites. Please review their policies before interacting.

Contact Us

If you have questions about this privacy policy, contact us via our GitHub Issues page or email us at support@passzap.com (if you set up a custom domain).

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. The latest version will always be available at this page, with the “Last updated” date at the top.
